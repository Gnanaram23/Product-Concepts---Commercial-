Evidence Synthesis

Focused review on burden of disease, epidemiology, unmet need, and cost-of-illness. Faster, question-driven approach
Lead: Medical Affairs / HEOR · Support: Clinical Research
Clinical Data

Phase I–III RCTs, CSRs, long-term follow-up, OLE studies, subgroup analyses, and patient-level IPD

Incidence, prevalence, DALYs, disease burden, unmet medical need — segmented by geography, line of therapy, subgroup

Indirect Comparison
NMA / ITC

Lead: Biostatistics / HEOR · Validate: HTA Specialists
Patient Evidence
PRO / QoL Data
Patient-Reported Outcomes, HRQoL instruments (EQ-5D, SF-36, FACT), mapping studies for utility values essential for QALY calculation
Lead: PRO Specialists / HEOR · Support: Clinical
Real-World

Registries, claims databases, EHR data, observational studies supplementing trial evidence for HTA and outcomes-based contracts

Europe — HTA Bodies
NICE
UK — National Institute for Health and Care Excellence
Technology Appraisal
Cost-utility analysis (CUA) framework. ICER threshold ~£20,000–£30,000/QALY (up to £50,000 for end-of-life). Requires STA/MTA dossier with economic model, NMA, EQ-5D utilities. Highly structured submission process with clear guidance on preferred methods.
GVD modules used: Clinical evidence · Economic model · NMA · PRO/QoL (EQ-5D) · BIM
HAS
France — Haute Autorité de Santé
Clinical Benefit Assessment
Two-stage process: SMR (clinical benefit rating) then ASMR (added value vs comparator, scale I–V). Does not require cost-effectiveness for initial review. Price negotiated with CEPS post-ASMR. ASMR I–III drives premium pricing; ASMR IV/V limits it.
GVD modules used: Clinical evidence · Comparator analysis · Disease background
IQWiG / G-BA
Germany — AMNOG Process
Benefit Assessment
AMNOG: G-BA defines appropriate comparator (zweckmäßige Komparator). IQWiG assesses additional benefit (major/considerable/minor/non-quantifiable). Direct head-to-head preferred; NMA accepted with justification. Price negotiated with GKV-Spitzenverband. No formal QALY threshold.
GVD modules used: Clinical evidence · NMA (with caveats) · Safety · PRO
EMA / EUnetHTA (JCA)
EU — Joint Clinical Assessment (EU HTA Regulation 2022/282)
Joint HTA
Mandatory Joint Clinical Assessment (JCA) for oncology and ATMPs from 2025. Single centralised evidence package submitted across all EU member states. National HTA bodies conduct economic assessment locally post-JCA. Requires EUnetHTA Core HTA format.
GVD modules used: All clinical modules · NMA · PICOS · Comparators
AIFA
Italy — Agenzia Italiana del Farmaco
Value-Based Pricing
Classifies drugs by therapeutic importance (Class A = fully reimbursed). Negotiates price-volume agreements, managed entry agreements (MEAs), risk-sharing, and outcome-based contracts. Clinical value + budget impact are central to price negotiation.
GVD modules used: Clinical · Economic · Budget impact · RWE
TLV
Sweden — Dental and Pharmaceutical Benefits Agency
CEA Assessment
Formal cost-effectiveness analysis required. Applies proportionality principle — higher ICER acceptable for severe diseases. NT-Council process for hospital medicines. Transparent ICER assessment with severity-tiered thresholds. Often referenced as a model CEA system.
GVD modules used: All modules including full economic model
ZIN (Zorginstituut Nederland)
Netherlands — Package Assessment
Conditional Reimbursement
Assesses necessary care (pakketbeheer). CEA threshold ~€80,000/QALY for severe disease. Conditional reimbursement (Voorwaardelijke Toelating) possible pending RWE collection. Lock-in reassessment after 4 years based on outcomes data.
GVD modules used: Clinical · Economic · RWE study design · BIM
AEM / GENESIS
Spain — Ministry of Health / CIPM
Efficiency Report
GENESIS report (hospital pharmacists network) assesses therapeutic value. CIPM (Interministerial Pricing Commission) sets national price. Autonomous communities (CCAA) may apply additional access restrictions. CEA/CUA required for premium pricing.
GVD modules used: Clinical · Economic model · BIM · Comparator analysis
North America — Payers & Assessment Bodies
ICER
USA — Institute for Clinical and Economic Review
Independent Assessment
Non-binding but highly influential. Publishes value-based price benchmarks ($100K–$150K/QALY threshold range). Used by PBMs, self-insured employers, and payers in contract negotiations. Rare disease programs use modified QALY or equal-value-of-life-years (evLYG) frameworks.
GVD modules used: Clinical · Economic model · NMA · BIM
CMS / Medicare
USA — Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services
Coverage & Reimbursement
NCDs and LCDs govern coverage. IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) enables Medicare price negotiation for high-spend drugs. Part B vs Part D designation is commercially critical. No formal QALY threshold; clinical effectiveness and budget impact are key levers.
GVD modules used: Clinical evidence · Safety · RWE · BIM
PBMs (CVS, Express Scripts, OptumRx)
USA — Pharmacy Benefit Managers
Payer / Formulary
Control formulary placement (Tier 1–4), prior authorisation criteria, and step therapy requirements for ~270M lives combined. Clinical value + net price (rebates) drive formulary decisions. P&T Committee reviews GVD clinical and comparative evidence.
GVD modules used: Clinical value narrative · Comparator data · Safety · BIM
CADTH / CDR + pCODR
Canada — Canadian Drug Review
Reimbursement Recommendation
CDR (Common Drug Review) for non-oncology; pCODR for oncology. Recommendations to provinces for formulary listing. Full pharmacoeconomic submission (CEA) required alongside clinical dossier. PMPRB sets ceiling price. Provincial variations in access post-recommendation.
GVD modules used: All modules · Pharmacoeconomic submission
Asia-Pacific & Emerging Markets
PBAC
Australia — Pharmaceutical Benefits Advisory Committee
PBS Listing
Recommends PBS listing based on clinical and cost-effectiveness. Formal CEA/CUA required; threshold not publicly stated but ~AUD 45,000–75,000/QALY. Risk-sharing agreements (RSAs) common for uncertainty. NMA required when no direct head-to-head vs comparator.
GVD modules used: All modules in PBAC submission format
NECA / HiRAS
South Korea — National Evidence-based Healthcare Collaborating Agency
NHI HTA
Mandatory HTA for NHI reimbursement listing. Full economic evaluation required. RSAs used for uncertainty. Korea-specific epidemiology and clinical practice data required — bridging studies may be needed to supplement global trial data.
GVD modules used: Clinical · Economic · Local epidemiology
NHSA / CDE
China — National Healthcare Security Administration
NRDL Negotiation
Annual NRDL (National Reimbursement Drug List) negotiation cycle. Pharmacoeconomic evaluation required. Price reductions of 50–70% are common at negotiation. China-specific clinical data increasingly required; bridging studies often needed for locally relevant evidence.
GVD modules used: Adapted clinical · Local RWE · Localised economic model
HTAi / EUnetHTA / WHO
Global — International HTA Network
Global Reference
HTAi facilitates knowledge-sharing across 50+ HTA bodies. NICE International advises LMICs. WHO EML listing uses clinical evidence packages. GVD serves as the master source document from which all country-level adaptations and submissions are derived.
GVD modules used: Clinical evidence core · Burden of disease · Safety
